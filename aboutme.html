Short Biography

  • Dr. Yanfang (Fanny) Ye is currently the Galassi Family Collegiate Professor in Computer Science and Engineering (Endowed Chair Full Professor) in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) and the Associate Director of Applied Analytics in the Lucy Family Institute for Data & Society at the University of Notre Dame. Before joining Notre Dame, she was the Theodore L. and Dana J. Schroeder Associate Professor in the Department of Computer and Data Sciences at Case Western Reserve University (CWRU); prviously, she was an Assistant Professor and then Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering at West Virginia University (WVU). She received her Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Department of Computer Science at Xiamen University in 2010. Before joining academia, she had six-year working experience in cybersecurity industry: she was the Principal Scientist in Comodo Security Solutions, Inc. (2010-2013) and formerly the Deputy Director R&D at Kingsoft Internet Security Corporation (2008-2010). Dr. Ye's research areas mainly include artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning (ML), data mining, cybersecurity, and public health, with focus on i) advancing knowledge and science in graph learning and trustworthy multimodal learning, ii) bridging AI/ML and cybersecurity concentrating on AI security, large-scale malware detection, and the study of the evolving underground ecosystem, and iii) developing AI and data-driven techniques to combat the opioid crisis and infectious disease outbreaks. Dr. Ye has had ~150 publications in her fields (e.g., ACM CSUR, IEEE TNNLS, IEEE TKDE, SIGKDD, ICDM, CIKM, WWW, NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, AAAI, IJCAI, USENIX Security, NDSS, ACSAC). By working with her team, Dr. Ye has received nine best paper awards, including the AAAI-DCAA 2023 Best Paper Runner-Up Award, the ACM CIKM 2021 Best Paper Award (Full Paper Track), the ACM CIKM 2021 Best Paper Runner-Up Award (Applied Paper Track), the AICS 2019 Challenge Problem Winner, the SIGKDD 2017 Best Paper Award and SIGKDD 2017 Best Student Paper Award (Applied Data Science Track), and the IEEE EISIC 2017 Best Paper Award. She has also received the Innovation Award (2020-2021) and the Research Award (2019-2020) at CWRU, the MetroLab Innovation of the Month (2020), the NSF Career Award (2019), the IJCAI Early Career Spotlight (2019), and the New Researcher of the Year Award (2016-2017) at WVU. As the Lead/Sole PI, she has received multiple awards from the NSF and DoJ/NIJ in support of her research. All these awards are highly competitive. Dr. Ye is an Associate Editor in IEEE Transaction on Big Data (TBD), IEEE Transaction on Dependable and Secure Computing (TDSC), and ACM Transactions on Privacy and Security (TOPS); she has also served as an Area Chair/SPC/PC of top-tier international conferences in her fields such as SIGKDD, AAAI, IJCAI, NeurIPS, ICDM, CIKM, SDM, PKDD, S&P, USENIX Security, ACSAC. She has also received the ICDM 2018 Outstanding Service Award.
